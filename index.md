## About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at Jagiellonian University Medical College working at the intersection of digital health, wearable technologies, and biostatistics. My research focuses on wearable-derived digital phenotyping, particularly the characterization of sleep, physical activity, and rest–activity rhythms and their associations with mental health outcomes. Through the application of advanced statistical methods to data from wearable devices and remote monitoring technologies, I aim to improve our understanding of behavioral and physiological patterns that may serve as digital markers of mental health and well-being.

## Research Interests

- **Digital health:** remote patient monitoring, digital therapeutics, digital biomarkers, digital phenotyping
- **Wearable devices:** actigraphy, sleep monitoring, physical activity tracking, passive sensing
- **Biostatistics:** longitudinal data analysis, mixed-effects models, missing data methods, clustering, explanatory modeling, causal inference
- **Electronic Health Records:** patient pathways, clinical phenotyping, healthcare utilization patterns
