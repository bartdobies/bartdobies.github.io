## About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. student at Jagiellonian University Medical College, where my research focuses on wearable-derived digital phenotyping of sleep, physical activity, and rest–activity rhythms in relation to mental health outcomes. My work lies at the intersection of digital health, wearable technologies, and biostatistics, with a particular interest in the analysis of longitudinal sensor data collected through wearable devices.

Before starting my Ph.D., I worked for more than 3 years as a Biostatistician at Sidekick Health, a digital therapeutics company, where I contributed to clinical trials, real-world evidence studies, and the statistical evaluation of digital health interventions. I also worked as a Student Research Assistant at Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin, supporting research projects in medicine and public health.

I received my M.Sc. degree in Statistics from Humboldt University of Berlin, specializing in Biostatistics and Data Science. My academic and professional experiences have shaped my interest in applying advanced statistical methods to digital health data to better understand human behavior, health outcomes, and patient trajectories.

## Research Interests

- **Wearable devices:** actigraphy, sleep monitoring, physical activity tracking, passive sensing
- **Digital health:** digital therapeutics, digital biomarkers
- **Biostatistics:** longitudinal data analysis, missing data methods, cluster analysis, explanatory modeling, causal inference
- **Electronic Health Records:** patient pathways, clinical phenotyping, healthcare utilization patterns
